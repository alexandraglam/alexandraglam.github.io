techrangers site redesign

Overview

The Techrangers team is comprised of talented students at the Center for Distributed Learning, who provide course development for faculty and web/application development for the University of Central Florida. While the Techrangers already had a website designed, it had not been updated since 2012. As a Hack Day project, I pitched the idea to redesign their current site to match the university’s overall theme and to provide future Techrangers a site where they can learn more about development.

duration

4 months

objective

To redesign website to be mobile friendly, accessible and to match the universities theme.

my role

UX/UI Designer & Developer

the challenge

The main challenge during the first phase of the redesign was meeting the University of Central Florida’s color scheme. The previous design did not take into consideration the university’s color scheme which made the website feel out of place. Another issue I faced was developing the website to be fully responsive and to meet W3C web and accessibility standards. The client also wanted to add a blog feature, tutorials, and update their content to meet the Techrangers mission statement.

the solution

After speaking to the Techrangers Team Lead, I created a user flow of the primary user of the website. Once I had a solid understanding of who the primary users of the site were, I was able to come up with design ideas for the site. I took into consideration the color scheme, the new features, and any accessibility concerns that came along the way.

research process

the goal

  1. Design a responsive website.
  2. Make website accessbile and up to W3C web standards.
  3. Updated website's content (text, profiles, tech times).
  4. Include a blog and tutorials component.
  5. Write clean code and provide a style guide for developers.

user interviews

I conducted a group interview with CDL developers, current Techrangers and their team leads to get their input on the new site design. We started by going over the old site pages to decide what we wanted to keep/remove. Once we had the pages up on the board, we grouped the pages that could be part of the same section (i.e. tutorials page merged in new blog page). We continued this process until there was less of a clutter in each page.

user flows

I learned the new website will be managed by the Techrangers in order to serve as a place where they can learn more about development and technical writing. Since I knew who the primary user of the site was, I created a user flow to pinpoint how to the user was most likely to navigate the site. As a secondary user, I decided to create a user persona of a Techranger prospect and how they would navigate the site. This was specially helpful for those who are looking to apply for a position as a Techranger.

design process

prototyping & iterations

low-fidelity prototype

I first used Adobe XD to design the low-fidelity prototype to determine information architecture, key features and functionalities, and user flows.

evaluating initial prototype

After designing the low-fidelity prototype, I met with the client to perform an evaluation of the designs. During this phase, I made several improvements, including:

  1. Updating content in each page to ensure language is consistent throughout each page of the site.
  2. Making sure content is reduced when screen size is below tablet size.
  3. Adding “services” section on the homepage.
  4. Removing carousel from “showcase” section and instead, add 2 rows with latest projects.
  5. Show 3 blog posts instead of 2 in the blog section of the homepage.
  6. Add more content under “join us” section of homepage.

high-fidelity prototypes

Once I fixed the issues from the low-fidelity prototypes, I moved to high-fidelity prototypes and went though various design iterations.
